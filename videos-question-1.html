Staffing: Â What priorities would you enact to support the teachers and staff in best serving students?

On Thursday, September 29, 2022, I participated in the Conversations with Candidates series at Hotel Carmichael and presented by the Carmel Education Foundation, the Carmel Public Library Foundation, and the OneZone Chamber of Commerce. Jack Russell was our gracious moderator.



Thanks to the Carmel Education Foundation, the Carmel Clay Library Foundation, and the OneZone Chamber of Commerce for organizing this candidate forum for our community. And a special thanks to our moderator Jack Russell for facilitating these conversations. Thank you.

One of my highest priorities is to ensure we continue to have a solid plan to recruit and retain the best teachers. According to our CCS Legislative Priorities letter, our nation is experiencing a shortage of teachers, low enrollment of students in university education programs, and only 1 in 6 students studying education pursue a career in teaching.

While we are fortunate to be fully staffed with teachers, we have a shortage of special education life skills teachers and instructional assistants.

If elected, I will prioritize partnering with our legislative leaders to ensure future salary increases for our teachers are competitive with other career choices.

Teaching is a higher calling. We educate our children to help them grow, support themselves, and be productive members of our society.

I will prioritize working with our community stakeholders so that future educators are inspired to the teaching profession. Our schools should be a model where teachers are respected, supported, and compensated well.

School Safety:Â  What do you think of the safety referendum implementation thus far, and where do you see opportunity?

On Thursday, September 29, 2022, I participated in the Conversations with Candidates series at Hotel Carmichael and presented by the Carmel Education Foundation, the Carmel Public Library Foundation, and the OneZone Chamber of Commerce. Jack Russell was our gracious moderator.



The implementation of the safety referendum is off to a good start.

  • We are now fully staffed with 21 school resource officers from the Carmel Police Department, which made it possible to start the new school year with a school resource officer in every school.

We have an opportunity to promote the mental health aspects of the safety referendum.

  • The partnership with St. Vincent to provide behavioral health services inside our schools come to mind. The ability for students to access these services in school is a big deal for working parents who may not have the flexibility to take frequent time off from work.
  • Parent Project on the county level is another project we should promote. The Parenting Project is an 11-week program designed specifically for parents of strong-willed children.

These two programs can serve as models for the rest of our Nation. We should continue to take a multi-stakeholder approach to safety and mental health.

We need to find creative ways to promote these programs.

This could include promoting them at:

  • Meet the Teacher night
  • Parent-teacher conferences
  • Sports games, and
  • Other in-person school events.
